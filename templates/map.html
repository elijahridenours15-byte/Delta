Field Flow
  1. Center the target under the crosshair or stop the cursor on the exact point you want to capture.
  2. Record latitude and longitude together, then log the MGRS and UTM readouts as backup references.
  3. Use the measure tool or the azimuth worksheet for route legs: kilometers for movement over 1 km, meters for the final 1000 m and final offset.
  4. Push the same point into GOTO, bookmarks, or PLANNING so the exact location stays reusable inside Tac Map.

Decimal Precision Reference

Latitude spacing stays consistent; longitude spacing tightens as you move away from the equator. Use this map's 6-decimal readout when you need the most precise notation.

0.001 deg ~ 111 m Use for coarse terrain association only.
0.0001 deg ~ 11 m Useful for building, road, or vehicle-sized targets.
0.00001 deg ~ 1.1 m Good for precise checkpoints and handoff points.
0.000001 deg ~ 0.11 m Use the full readout when you want the tightest coordinate notation available on this page.
