FILE 01 — THE CRUCIFIXION: HOW JESUS DIED

HOLY SCRIPTURE — EYEWITNESS ACCOUNT

When they came to the place called The Skull, they crucified Him there, along with the criminals — one on His right and the other on His left. Jesus said, "Father, forgive them, for they do not know what they are doing." ... It was now about the sixth hour, and darkness came over the whole land until the ninth hour, for the sun stopped shining. And the curtain of the temple was torn in two. Jesus called out with a loud voice, "Father, into your hands I commit my spirit." When He had said this, He breathed His last.
HOLY SCRIPTURE — MESSIANIC PROPHECY
Isaiah 53:3-7 — Written ~700 Years Before the Crucifixion
He was despised and rejected by mankind, a man of suffering, and familiar with pain. Surely He took up our pain and bore our suffering, yet we considered Him punished by God, stricken by Him, and afflicted. But He was pierced for our transgressions, He was crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was on Him, and by His wounds we are healed. We all, like sheep, have gone astray, each of us has turned to our own way; and the LORD has laid on Him the iniquity of us all. HOLY SCRIPTURE — PROPHECY FULFILLED
Psalm 22:16-18 — Written ~1000 Years Before Crucifixion Was Invented
Dogs surround me, a pack of villains encircles me; they pierce my hands and my feet. All my bones are on display; people stare and gloat over me. They divide my clothes among them and cast lots for my garment. — King David wrote this approximately 1,000 years before the Romans invented crucifixion. Every detail was fulfilled at the cross of Jesus.
HOLY SCRIPTURE — MEDICAL DETAIL
John 19:33-35 — Proof of Physical Death
But when they came to Jesus and found that He was already dead, they did not break His legs. Instead, one of the soldiers pierced Jesus' side with a spear, bringing a sudden flow of blood and water. The man who saw it has given testimony, and his testimony is true. — The separation of blood and water is medical evidence of death by cardiac rupture or pericardial effusion, confirming Jesus physically died on the cross.
HISTORICAL SOURCE — ROMAN RECORD
Tacitus, Annals 15.44 (~116 AD) — Roman Historian
"Christus, from whom the name had its origin, suffered the extreme penalty during the reign of Tiberius at the hands of one of our procurators, Pontius Pilatus, and a most mischievous superstition, thus checked for the moment, again broke out not only in Judaea, the first source of the evil, but even in Rome." — Cornelius Tacitus, Roman senator and historian. This is a non-Christian Roman source confirming Jesus was executed under Pontius Pilate.
HISTORICAL SOURCE — JEWISH RECORD
Babylonian Talmud, Sanhedrin 43a — Jewish Court Records
"On the eve of Passover Yeshu was hanged." — The Babylonian Talmud, written by Jesus' opponents, independently confirms His execution took place at Passover — exactly as the Gospels record. Even those who rejected Him never denied that He was killed.
HISTORICAL SOURCE — JEWISH HISTORIAN
Josephus, Antiquities 18.3.3 (~93 AD)
"About this time there lived Jesus, a wise man. For he was one who performed surprising deeds and was a teacher of such people as accept the truth gladly. He won over many Jews and many of the Greeks. And when, upon the accusation of the principal men among us, Pilate had condemned him to a cross, those who had first come to love him did not cease." — Flavius Josephus, Jewish historian writing for Rome.

FILE 02 — THE BURIAL: JESUS IN THE TOMB

HOLY SCRIPTURE — EYEWITNESS ACCOUNT
Matthew 27:57-66 — The Secured Tomb
As evening approached, there came a rich man from Arimathea, named Joseph, who had himself become a disciple of Jesus. Going to Pilate, he asked for Jesus' body, and Pilate ordered that it be given to him. Joseph took the body, wrapped it in a clean linen cloth, and placed it in his own new tomb that he had cut out of the rock. He rolled a big stone in front of the entrance to the tomb and went away. The next day the chief priests and the Pharisees went to Pilate. "Sir," they said, "we remember that while He was still alive that deceiver said, 'After three days I will rise again.' So give the order for the tomb to be made secure." So they went and made the tomb secure by putting a seal on the stone and posting the guard.
HOLY SCRIPTURE — PROPHECY FULFILLED
Isaiah 53:9 — Written ~700 Years Before
"He was assigned a grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death, though he had done no violence, nor was any deceit in his mouth." — Jesus was crucified alongside criminals (the wicked) but buried in the private tomb of the wealthy Joseph of Arimathea (the rich). Fulfilled to the letter.

FILE 04 — THE EVIDENCE: WHY IT MATTERS

EVIDENCE SUMMARY
The Empty Tomb — A Fact No One Denied
Both the Roman authorities and Jewish leaders had every reason to produce the body of Jesus and end Christianity on the spot. They never did. Instead, Matthew 28:11-15 records that the guards were bribed to say the disciples stole the body — an admission that the tomb was empty. The enemies of Jesus never claimed the tomb still held His body. They only tried to explain why it was empty.
EVIDENCE SUMMARY
The Martyrdom of the Apostles — They Died for What They Saw
People will die for something they believe to be true. But people do not die for something they know to be a lie. Nearly every one of the apostles was executed for proclaiming that Jesus rose from the dead. Peter was crucified upside down. James was beheaded. Paul was beheaded. Thomas was speared. Not one of them recanted. If they had stolen the body or fabricated the resurrection, at least one would have confessed under threat of death. None did — because they had seen Him alive.
EVIDENCE SUMMARY
The Transformation of James — Jesus' Own Brother
During Jesus' ministry, His own brother James did not believe in Him (John 7:5). After the resurrection, James became the leader of the Jerusalem church and was martyred for his faith. What could convince a man's own skeptical brother that he was the Son of God? James saw the risen Jesus (1 Corinthians 15:7).
EVIDENCE SUMMARY
The Conversion of Paul — From Persecutor to Apostle
Saul of Tarsus actively hunted and killed Christians. He had every motive to destroy the resurrection claim. Instead, he encountered the risen Jesus on the road to Damascus (Acts 9), became the Apostle Paul, wrote 13 books of the New Testament, and was eventually beheaded for his faith. No psychological explanation accounts for this transformation — only the resurrection does.
EVIDENCE SUMMARY
Women as First Witnesses — Proof It Wasn't Fabricated
In the 1st-century Jewish and Roman world, the testimony of women was not accepted in court. If the disciples invented the resurrection story, they would never have chosen women as the first witnesses — it would have destroyed their credibility. The fact that all four Gospels name women as the first witnesses is powerful evidence that the authors were recording what actually happened, not crafting a believable lie.
EVIDENCE SUMMARY
The Explosion of the Church — From 12 to Billions
Within weeks of the resurrection, in the very city where Jesus was publicly executed, thousands believed. Within 300 years, the Roman Empire that crucified Jesus officially became Christian. Today, over 2.4 billion people follow Jesus Christ. This global movement was launched by a handful of terrified fishermen who claimed they saw a dead man walk out of His grave. Either they saw it, or history has no explanation for what happened next.
